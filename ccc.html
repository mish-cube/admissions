College and Course Choices

Let's briefly interpret the above data. These are from 2025 Cambridge admissions.

Firstly, the outliers. Trinity has a well earned remarkable reputation for Maths (which leaks into Physics), massive skewing applications. The least popular colleges - Hughes Hall, St Edmund's, and Wolfson - are Postgraduate / Mature Student colleges. We can ignore these. Generally speaking, there's no other crazy differences between the colleges.

My advice would be simply to choose a subject that you're passionate about, and a college that looks cool! Size, proximity to city centre / proximity to lecture theatres, and general reputation can all come into play. I went to a tiny college (Corpus Christ). It was cosy and central. Supermarkets were close by but the noise from tourists on the street was immense. In third year I had to bike 15 minutes for my Physics lectures and labs. I loved it.

Having said that, some courses are definitely more competitive than others (e.g. Cambridge 2025, above: Maths has a 1/8 admission rate compared to 1/5 for Natural Sciences) and it's worth being aware of this when you apply.

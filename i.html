Interview

General Advice

Interview questions do not have a right answer.

The goal is not to be as correct as possible.

There is no checklist of "things the student should have mentioned".

An interview is a conversation, and the objective of the professors is to gain an understanding of how you think, and how you communicate those thoughts with others. The ability to explain a concept well to another person is a great marker of your own understanding, and communication with others is an essential part of any field.

I've brought in some subject experts to give more context and advice for interviews across a variety of subjects. This should cover a reasonable enough range so you can infer how your subject interview will roughly look like, even if it's not actually on the list.

Enjoy, and one more thing - you may get asked questions about things from your personal statement. These are the easiest questions to prepare for and ace, so don't be caught slacking!

Natural Sciences (Bio/Chem)

Hello, I’m Yuhang. I studied Natural Sciences (ending up specialising in Chemistry) at the University of Cambridge from 2017 – 2021.

I applied through the biological stream of Natural Sciences and was accepted into King’s College. It has been a crazy, busy, intense but really fulfilling time. The teaching is fast. It takes much more self-motivation and self-study but also you are surrounded by wonderful people in the same boat and there’s plenty of fun too.

After Cambridge, I worked in the biotech industry for 3 years, designing, building and testing medical and diagnostic devices. In my spare time, I also tutor GCSE and A-level sciences and maths and have been doing this for many years. In 2024, I went back into academia, starting a PhD in Interdisciplinary Biosciences at the University of Oxford which has been great fun so far.

The Oxbridge admissions process can be nebulous and intimidating. But hopefully I can share a little about what to expect and how to prepare for your admissions and interviews.

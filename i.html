General Advice

Interview questions do not have a right answer.

The goal is not to be as correct as possible.

There is no checklist of "things the student should have mentioned".

An interview is a conversation, and the objective of the professors is to gain an understanding of how you think, and how you communicate those thoughts with others. Partially because the ability to explain a concept well to another person is a great marker of your own understanding, but also communication with others is an essential part of any field, even the purest of mathematics.

I'm very fortunate to have some subject experts on hand here to give context and advice for interviews across a variety of subjects. Enjoy!

STEP

Website, Syllabus, and Past Papers.

Lord have mercy on you.

Step is fiendish and even the most successful candidates find it taxing. The good news is, there is more targeted support for STEP than for the other admission tests.

The official support programme is one, this book of worked answers another, and this website of questions filtered by topic a third.

STEP looks like something like this:

Explain why \( \sum_{r=n}^{\infty} \frac{1}{r^2} > \int_{n}^{\infty} \frac{dx}{x^2} > \sum_{r=n+1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{r^2} \)
